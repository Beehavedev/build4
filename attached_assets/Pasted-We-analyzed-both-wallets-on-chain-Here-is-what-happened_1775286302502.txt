HOW THE ATTACK WORKS TODAR

ERC 7702 was introduced with the Pectra upgrade. It allows a normal wallet to delegate its execution to a smart contract by signing one authorization. Once signed the delegate  contract gains full control. It can transfer BNB. It can transfer tokens. It can set approvals. It can do anything your private key could do. You do not need to sign anything else after that initial authorization. One signature is enough.

CrimeEnjoyer variant specifically has a fallback function. This means even if you send new BNB to your wallet it will be automatically forwarded to the attacker in the same transaction. The delegation is still active right now.

HOW YOU SIGNED IT WITHOUT KNOWING

MetaMask and other wallet UIs did not properly flag ERC 7702 authorization payloads when BSC first enabled support. Wintermute's research confirmed that over 80% of ERC 7702 delegations in the wild were linked to this malicious script family. What you saw in MetaMask looked like a normal transaction confirmation. Behind it was an ERC 7702  authorization that delegated your wallet. A documented case on Ethereum showed a user losing $146,551 through the same method.
